About

I'm a final-year Biomedical Engineering student at Dhanalakshmi Srinivasan College of Engineering and Technology, specializing in medical device design, rehabilitation engineering, and wearable health tech. Most of what I know, I learned by building: wiring an ESP32 at 2am before a hackathon demo, calibrating an ultrasonic sensor until the numbers finally made sense, or debugging why a gyroscope integration drifted over a 10-minute walk.

That hands-on habit runs across instrumentation, embedded systems, IoMT, and cloud-connected healthcare tools — from a wearable EEG system that won a statewide hackathon, to a patent-pending incubator controller, to an Android app that watches ICU staff sanitize their hands. I'm currently interning at Christian Medical College, Vellore, continuing to work at the intersection of engineering and patient care.
